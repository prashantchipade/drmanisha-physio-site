Geriatric physiotherapy

Geriatric physiotherapy is a specialized field focusing on older adults, addressing age-related conditions like reduced mobility, muscle weakness, balance problems, and chronic pain. It involves personalized treatment plans with exercises to improve strength, flexibility, and balance, manual therapy for pain, and strategies for fall prevention. The primary goals are to enhance mobility, maintain independence, and improve the overall quality of life for seniors

Common conditions treated

  • Arthritis, osteoporosis, and other musculoskeletal problems.
  • Balance disorders and reduced coordination, which increase the risk of falls.
  • Muscle weakness, reduced flexibility, and stiffness.
  • Chronic pain and the recovery process after surgery, stroke, or injury.
  • Neurological conditions like Parkinson's disease

Benefits for seniors

Improved Mobility:Helps individuals move more freely and perform daily activities like walking and climbing stairs.

Enhanced Independence:Supports the ability to perform everyday tasks without assistance, boosting confidence.

Pain Management:Reduces chronic pain and discomfort associated with age-related conditions.

Fall Prevention:Addresses balance issues and strength deficits to mitigate the risk of falls, which can have severe consequences for older adults.

Better Quality of Life:Contributes to overall well-being and helps seniors remain active and engaged.
