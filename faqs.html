Frequently Asked Questions - Answers to common patient questions.

Q. What is physiotherapy?
Physiotherapy helps restore movement and function when affected by injury.
Q. Do I need a doctor’s referral?
No, you can book directly without a referral in most cases.
Q. What should I bring to my appointment?
Bring previous reports, X-rays, MRI and any prescriptions.
Q. What conditions can a physiotherapist treat?
Musculoskeletal: Neck and back pain, arthritis, joint pain, ligament and bone issues, and post-trauma stiffness.
Neurological: Conditions like stroke, Parkinson's disease, and multiple sclerosis to improve function and mobility.
Other:Issues arising from various injuries or surgeries.
Q. Will my condition relapse after taking Class IV Laser treatment?
Laser therapy, especially Class IV, is a boon for physiotherapy as its light energy can penetrate more than 5 cm into the body to heal deep-seated injuries—an alternative that previously required surgery. Such injuries often result from factors like excess weight, nutritional deficiencies, poor lifestyle, or faulty ergonomics. However, unless these underlying issues are corrected after healing, there is a high risk of re-injury at the same site.
Physiotherapy sessions—whether Laser, Ultrasound, IFT, or any other modality—are designed to help you recover from injury and break the cycle of pain. It’s much like taking antibiotics for an infection: antibiotics help your body fight off the infection, but once the course is complete, it’s up to you to maintain hygiene, maintain healthy lifestyle to prevent reinfection.
Q. What can I expect during a session?
Initial Assessment: A thorough examination of your medical history, symptoms, and goals.
Treatment Plan: A personalized plan with specific interventions.
Interventions: May include therapeutic exercises, manual therapy, laser therapy, ultrasound therapy, electrotherapy or decompression therapy to manage pain and inflammation, and education on posture and ergonomics.
Progress Monitoring: Your progress is continuously monitored, and the treatment plan is adjusted as needed.
Q. What should I do to prepare for a session?
Clothing: Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing that allows for easy movement, and athletic shoes if exercises are involved.
Documents: Bring any relevant medical reports and a list of your symptoms and goals for the session.
Q. What are common concerns about physiotherapy?
Pain: Physiotherapy should not be painful; while some mild discomfort might occur, you should communicate any pain to your physiotherapist so they can adjust the treatment.
Duration: Physiotherapy is often an ongoing, comprehensive process, not a one-time treatment, with sessions typically lasting 30-60 minutes, though the initial assessment can be longer.
Q. How long will physiotherapy take to work?
The total duration of treatment depends on the specific condition, its severity, and how consistently you follow the plan. While some people notice improvements within a few sessions, others with more complex or chronic issues may need several weeks or months of consistent therapy
Q. What are the benefits of physiotherapy?
Key benefits of physiotherapy include:
Pain relief: Helps reduce chronic and acute pain without relying heavily on medication.
Improved mobility: Increases flexibility and range of motion, making daily activities easier.
Enhanced balance: Strengthens muscles and improves stability, reducing the risk of falls.
Injury prevention: Teaches proper techniques and body mechanics to avoid future injuries.
Non-invasive approach: Offers an alternative to surgery for many conditions

Q. What is meant by treatment plan ?
The treatment plan is a blend of Laser and Electro therapy, designed to effectively alleviate pain and expedite recovery. Each individual’s condition necessitates a tailored treatment strategy. Factors such as age, type of injury, overall health, and the physical demands of your occupation all play a role in shaping the treatment plan for effective and long-term recovery. The duration of each therapy is carefully calibrated to deliver optimal doses for maximum healing. Each treatment plan is grounded in extensive experience dealing with various conditions and understanding how your body responds to each therapy.
Q. Why is it crucial to consistently follow the doctor’s recommended sessions without any breaks?
The human body requires a specific duration for ideal recovery. The initial sessions are structured to alleviate pain and break the cycle of discomfort. The following sessions support the body’s healing process, speeding up recuperation and fostering complete recovery. If there are disruptions, the body might revert to its defensive mechanism, possibly leading you back into the cycle of pain. Hence, it is extremely important to stick to the sessions as prescribed by the doctor.
Q. In the course of physiotherapy, will the treatment plan solely consist of exercises, or will it also include other forms of treatment or medication?
Within the scope of physiotherapy, subsequent to a comprehensive problem assessment, recommendations may encompass either a regimen of exercises or the utilization of physical therapies. Among the physical therapies available, we offer both electrotherapy and advanced photobiomodulation therapy. These modalities are designed to effectively address the underlying causes of issues such as muscle injuries, strains, and spasms. Following these therapy sessions, a customized exercise plan will be prescribed to mitigate the risk of recurrence and to foster enduring resolution of the concern. We prioritize minimizing the use of medication whenever feasible, as it generally offers only transient relief.
