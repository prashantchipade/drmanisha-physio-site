Our Services

Back & Neck Pain

Common reasons for back & neck pain include muscle strain, disk damage, and some chronic conditions, such as fractures due to osteoporosis.

Knee Pain

Knee pain may be the result of an injury, such as a ruptured ligament or torn cartilage. Medical conditions — including arthritis, gout and infections — also can cause knee pain..

Shoulder Pain

Shoulder problems can cause a variety of symptoms, including pain, weakness, and limited movement. The location and type of pain can help a doctor diagnose the issue

Joint Pain & Arthritis Care

Arthritis is a general term for joint pain and inflammation, affecting one or more joints and causing pain, stiffness, and reduced movement. It can be caused by various factors, including wear and tear, autoimmune conditions, and infections, leading to a wide range of symptoms.

Hip Pain

Hip pain is a common symptom that can be caused by anything from sports injuries to arthritis. if the pain persists or gets worse, other treatments may be necessary.

Foot & Ankle Pain

Foot and ankle pain can stem from various factors, including injuries, arthritis, overuse, and underlying medical conditions. Common causes include sprains, strains, Achilles tendonitis, plantar fasciitis, and flat feet. Seeking medical attention is recommended if pain persists, worsens..

Spinal Injuries

Spinal cord injuries can result from trauma, such as accidents or falls, or from non-traumatic causes like tumors or infections. These injuries can range in severity, causing a variety of symptoms including pain, weakness, loss of sensation, and even paralysis.

Bell's palsy

Bell’s palsy temporarily weakens or paralyzes facial muscles. People with Bell’s palsy develop a droopy appearance on one — or sometimes both — sides of their face.

Vertigo Management

Physiotherapy plays a crucial role in treating vertigo, particularly for conditions like Benign Paroxysmal Positional Vertigo (BPPV). It involves repositioning exercises, such as the Epley maneuver, and vestibular rehabilitation programs to improve balance, gaze stability, and reduce dizziness

Postural Correction & Ergonomics

Desk job programs and posture retraining.

Post-Surgery Rehabilitation

Progressive strengthening and mobility protocols.

Sports Injury Management

Injury assessment, taping, return-to-sport.

Geriatric Physiotherapy

Geriatric physiotherapy is a specialized area of physical therapy focused on the unique needs of older adults, aiming to maintain and improve their mobility, strength, and overall function. It addresses age-related changes in the body, such as muscle weakness, joint stiffness, and balance problems..
